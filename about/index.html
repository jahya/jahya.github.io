About

Andrew McWilliams is an art and technology program director specializing in production, implementation and community facilitation of technology-based art practice.

He is a founder and director of Thoughtworks Arts, a suite of programs researching emerging technologies via collaborations between groundbreaking artists and technologists. He is a founding member of ClimateAction.tech, a global community initiative to enable technology professionals to take action on climate and sustainability. As an independent artist McWilliams has created work which explores climate, technology, perception and personal health, producing and exhibiting work in Europe, India and North America.

McWilliams' work has been featured in Forbes, Fast Company, ThinkProgress and CLOT Magazine, and he has spoken at TEDx Vilnius, Aaron Swartz Day in San Francisco and Creative Tech Week in New York.

Thoughtworks Arts has been featured in The New York Times, Engadget, WIRED, Forbes, Smithsonian Magazine, and has been credited on a STARTS Prize Honorable Mention and a winning Prix Ars Electronica Award of Distinction in Digital Music.

ClimateAction.tech won a Prix Ars Electronica Award for Digital Humanity for the community-incubated Branch Magazine project.
